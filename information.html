What is Leprosy?

An infectious and chronic disease that is curable. It mainly causes skin lesions and nerve damage. It is caused by a slow-growing type of bacteria called Mycobacterium Leprae. Hansen's Disease is named after the scientist who discovered Mycobacterium, Armauer Hansen.

Those Affected

20,000 cases in the U.S. each year, 9% global mortality rate, People in low income countries are more affected by the disease.

Transmission

The infection spreads from person to person by nasal secretions or droplets. Leprosy rarely spreads from chimpanzees, mangabey monkeys, and nine-banded armadillos to humans by droplets or direct contact.

Symptoms

Skin lesions, muscle weakness, facial/body deformities.

Treatment

Antibiotics & inflammatory relief pills

Prevention

Keep a good hygiene, Make sure to drink purified water, Stay away from armadillos who often carry the parasites
