What is Hansen's?

Hansen's Disease, otherwise known as leprosy, is a chronic, curable infectious disease that is prevalent in lower income countries and southern California. It is transferred by bacteria called Mycobacterium leprae.

How Common is Hansen's?

There are approximately 20,000 cases of H.D. in the United States per year, and 200,000 cases worldwide. Hansen's Disease is highly contagious and spreads through contact with nasal secretions (mucus) of someone's who is infected. Someone can carry the disease for up to 20 years without showing any symptoms.
